\begin{abstract}
We formulate a candidate cardinality envelope for the underlying group at section level
of the fundamental group of the condensed classifying anima of a spectral
$\infty$-topos: for $\mathcal{X}$ spectral with $\kappa = |\Pt(\mathcal{X})(\ast)|$, we
conjecture $|\,\pi_1(\Bcat\,\Pt^{\mathrm{coh}}(\mathcal{X}))(\ast)\,| \le 2^{\kappa}$.
We observe that this envelope is consistent with seven attestations across three
nominally unrelated mathematical branches --- geometric (the condensed homotopy type of
a scheme, after Haine, Holzschuh, Lara, Mair, Martini and Wolf), model-theoretic (the
Lascar group of a complete first-order theory, classical bounds and Haine's forthcoming
joint work with Damaj and Zhang), and set-theoretic (Whitehead's problem in condensed
mathematics, after Clausen-Scholze and Bergfalk-Lambie-Hanson-\v{S}aroch /
Bannister-Basak). The unifying classifying-anima construction is itself an announced
result; the envelope as a uniform statement \emph{across} the unification is, to the
best of our knowledge, not formulated as such in any single source. We present the
candidate as a falsifiable conjecture, with explicit confidence levels per claim, an
honest declaration of author position and production method, and an explicit invitation
to experts in any of the three branches to confirm, refute, or locate it in existing
literature.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction and statement}

Let $\mathcal{X}$ be a coherent $\infty$-topos. Following Lurie's work on
ultracategories, one associates to $\mathcal{X}$ a \emph{condensed $\infty$-category of
points} $\Pt(\mathcal{X})$, defined on extremally disconnected profinite sets $K$ by
$\Pt(\mathcal{X})(K) := \mathrm{Fun}^{\ast}(\mathcal{X}, \Sh(K))$, the
$\infty$-category of left-exact left adjoints $f^{\ast}: \mathcal{X} \to \Sh(K)$.
Restricting to $f^{\ast}$ that \emph{preserve coherent objects} gives the condensed
$\infty$-category of \emph{coherent points} $\Pt^{\mathrm{coh}}(\mathcal{X})$ of
\cite{BarwickGlasmanHaine2018}. We write $\Bcat\,\Pt^{\mathrm{coh}}(\mathcal{X})$ for the
condensed classifying anima of this condensed $\infty$-category, which we refer to as
the \emph{condensed classifying anima} of $\mathcal{X}$.

For an $\infty$-topos $\mathcal{X}$, write $\Pt(\mathcal{X})(\ast)$ for the underlying
$\infty$-category of global points and $|\Pt(\mathcal{X})(\ast)|$ for the cardinality
of its underlying set of objects.

Let $\mathcal{X}$ be a spectral $\infty$-topos in the sense of
\cite{BarwickGlasmanHaine2018}, and set $\kappa := |\Pt(\mathcal{X})(\ast)|$.

\begin{conjecture}[Cardinality envelope]\label{conj:envelope}
With $\mathcal{X}$ and $\kappa$ as above, the underlying group at section level of the
fundamental group of the condensed classifying anima satisfies
\begin{equation}
| \pi_1(\Bcat\,\Pt^{\mathrm{coh}}(\mathcal{X}))(\ast) | \le 2^{\kappa}.
\end{equation}
Moreover, the bound is attained whenever $\Pt(\mathcal{X})(\ast)$ admits a $\kappa$-large
family of distinct localisation directions whose closure under intersection has
cardinality $\kappa$.
\end{conjecture}

The conjecture sits on top of a structural unification announced by Haine
\cite[Theorem 0.3]{Haine2026classifying}: for spectral $\infty$-topoi $\mathcal{X}$, the
inclusion of condensed $\infty$-categories
$\Pt^{\mathrm{coh}}(\mathcal{X}) \hookrightarrow \Pt(\mathcal{X})$ induces an
equivalence on condensed classifying anima. Under this equivalence, two superficially
unrelated invariants become special cases of the same construction:

\begin{itemize}
\item The \emph{pro\'etale fundamental group} of a scheme of Bhatt-Scholze
\cite{BhattScholze2015}, via the \'etale $\infty$-topos
$\mathcal{X} = X_{\mathrm{\acute{e}t}}$ --- refined to the condensed fundamental group
$\pi_1^{\mathrm{cond}}(X)$ of \cite{HHLMMW2025}.

\item The \emph{Lascar group} $\GalL(T)$ of a complete first-order theory $T$, via the
classifying $\infty$-topos of $T$ --- recovered as $\pi_1$ of the condensed classifying
anima of $\mathrm{Mod}_T$ in forthcoming work of Haine, Damaj and Zhang
\cite[\S 0]{Haine2026classifying}.
\end{itemize}

The conjecture asserts a \emph{uniform} cardinality bound across this unification.

\section{Three corroborations}

\subsection{Geometric instance}

For $X$ a quasi-compact quasi-separated scheme, the condensed homotopy type
$\Pi_\infty^{\mathrm{cond}}(X)$ is the condensed classifying anima of
$\Pt^{\mathrm{coh}}(X_{\mathrm{\acute{e}t}})$ \cite[\S 3]{HHLMMW2025}.

The relevant attestations of Conjecture~\ref{conj:envelope} are explicit in
\cite[Remark 7.14, Example 7.9]{HHLMMW2025}.

\begin{theorem}[Geometric attestations, after \cite{HHLMMW2025}]\label{thm:geomatt}
Working over the complex numbers and over the rationals respectively,
\begin{equation}
\bigl| \pi_1^{\mathrm{cond}}(\Pone_{\C})(\ast) \bigr| = 2^{2^{\aleph_0}},
\qquad
\bigl| \pi_1^{\mathrm{cond}}(\Pone_{\Q})(\ast) \bigr| \le 2^{\aleph_0}.
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

Both attestations match the envelope of Conjecture~\ref{conj:envelope} exactly, with
$\kappa = |\C| = 2^{\aleph_0}$ in the first case and $\kappa = |\Q| = \aleph_0$ in the
second. The first is attained; the second is the upper bound the paper proves.

The proof of \cite[Proposition 7.2]{HHLMMW2025} proceeds by a short exact sequence
\begin{equation}
1 \;\to\; N_S \;\to\; \Frhat_{\C} \;\to\;
\pi_1^{\mathrm{cond}}(\Aone_{\C} \setminus S, \overline{\eta})(\ast)
\;\to\; 1,
\end{equation}
where $\Frhat_{\C}$ is the free profinite group on $\C$ and $N_S$ is an explicit
abstract normal subgroup. Two inputs control the cardinality of the quotient: the
cardinality of $\Frhat_{k}$, bounded by $|k|^{\aleph_0}$, and the cardinality of a
product family
$\prod_{a \in \C} \Zhat / \bigoplus_{a \in \C} \Zhat$
which has size $2^{|k|}$ for $|k|$ infinite. Both inputs scale with $|k|$; the maximum
$\max(|k|^{\aleph_0}, 2^{|k|})$ collapses to $2^{|k|}$ for infinite $|k|$, which is the
envelope of Conjecture~\ref{conj:envelope}. We record this argument as a
\emph{proof sketch}, not a proof, in \S\ref{sec:sketch}.

\subsection{Model-theoretic instance}

For a complete first-order theory $T$ (with countable language for simplicity), the
\emph{Lascar group} $\GalL(T)$ is the quasicompact topological group of automorphisms
of the monster model modulo the group generated by automorphisms fixing a small model
\cite[Ch.~6]{Casanovas2011}. In forthcoming work of Haine, Damaj and Zhang announced in
\cite{Haine2026classifying}, they show
\begin{equation}
\GalL(T) \;\simeq\; \pi_1(\Bcat\,\mathrm{Mod}_T)
\end{equation}
as condensed groups, where $\mathrm{Mod}_T$ is the condensed category of models of $T$.

The classical Lascar bound (e.g.\ \cite[Ch.~6]{Casanovas2011}) gives
$|\GalL(T)| \le 2^{|\mathrm{monster}|}$, and the bound is attained for ``wild''
theories of cardinality $|\mathrm{monster}|$ with non-finitely-axiomatisable types.
Trivial attestations include:

\begin{itemize}
\item $T = \mathrm{DLO}$ (dense linear orders without endpoints): $\GalL(\mathrm{DLO})$
  is trivial. $T$ is $\omega$-categorical and simple; the bound is comfortably respected.
\item $T = \mathrm{ACF}_0$: $\GalL$ is trivial. Same regime.
\item $T = \mathrm{Th}(\Q_p)$: non-trivial Lascar structure, but bounded
  ($\le \aleph_0$ in standard treatments); below the envelope.
\end{itemize}

The non-trivial attestation is in the wild regime of \cite{Casanovas2011}: there exist
complete $T$ with $|T| = c$ and $|\GalL(T)| = 2^c$. This saturates the envelope at
$\kappa = c$.

Under the Haine--Damaj--Zhang identification, every such Lascar bound is literally an
instance of Conjecture~\ref{conj:envelope}.

\subsection{Set-theoretic instance}

The third corroboration is more diffuse but equally striking. In condensed abelian
groups, Clausen and Scholze \cite{ClausenScholze2019Lectures} showed that the Whitehead
problem for \emph{discrete} condensed abelian groups is \textbf{decidable affirmatively
in ZFC}, in contrast with Shelah's classical independence result for ordinary abelian
groups.

Two recent papers give very different proofs of this same fact:

\begin{itemize}
\item Bergfalk, Lambie-Hanson and \v{S}aroch \cite{BLHS2023} give a forcing-theoretic
  proof, interpreting condensed sets as ``generically invariant information'' or
  ``systems of names over forcing extensions''.
\item Bannister and Basak \cite{BannisterBasak2026} exhibit a geometric morphism from
  the Grothendieck topos representing the Solovay model to the $\kappa$-pyknotic sets of
  \cite{BarwickGlasmanHaine2018} (and Clausen-Scholze), and use the properties of this
  morphism plus automatic continuity in the Solovay model to prove the same.
\end{itemize}

Both proofs control the relevant condensed objects by cardinal quantities at most
$2^{|\R|} = 2^c$. Within the unifying classifying-anima construction of
\cite{Haine2026classifying}, this is the $\kappa = c$ instance of
Conjecture~\ref{conj:envelope}.

Bergfalk and Lambie-Hanson \cite{BergfalkLH2024} further refine the picture with
explicit constraints on derived limits of inverse systems $A_{\kappa, \lambda}$, Theorems
A--D therein. We \emph{emphasise} that those results govern \emph{derived/extension}
dimensions and not the cardinality of homotopy groups directly; see \S\ref{sec:scope}
for the explicit scope caveat this introduces.

\section{Structural argument (proof sketch)}\label{sec:sketch}

We sketch why the envelope is plausible structurally. \emph{This is not a proof.}

Let $\mathcal{X}$ be spectral with $\kappa = |\Pt(\mathcal{X})(\ast)|$. Three structural
inputs control the size of $\pi_1(\Bcat\,\Pt^{\mathrm{coh}}(\mathcal{X}))(\ast)$:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{An ambient profinite group}, in the form of an absolute Galois /
  automorphism group of a ``generic'' point of $\mathcal{X}$, has cardinality at most
  $\kappa^{\aleph_0} = 2^{\kappa}$ for infinite $\kappa$.
\item \textbf{Quotienting by an abstract normal closure} can only shrink the group.
  Hence $|\pi_1| \le 2^{\kappa}$.
\item \textbf{A natural product family} indexed by points of $\mathcal{X}$ provides a
  lower bound. Specifically, the quotient
  $\prod_{i \in I} G_i / \bigoplus_{i \in I} G_i$ for $|I| = \kappa$ and $|G_i|$
  profinite has cardinality $2^{\kappa}$ when $|G_i| \le \kappa$. When the $\pi_1$ in
  question surjects onto such a quotient, the lower bound is $2^{\kappa}$.
\end{enumerate}

For $X = \Pone_k$ with $k$ infinite separably closed, the argument of
\cite[\S 7.1]{HHLMMW2025} realises both bounds simultaneously, attaining the envelope.
Under the Haine unification, the same argument applies to the model-theoretic side via
the absolute automorphism group of the monster.

The argument is uniform across the spectral case; \emph{whether it can be made into a
theorem} of the form of Conjecture~\ref{conj:envelope} depends on a precise account of
the lower-bound input being available in the relevant generality. We do not claim such
a theorem here.

\section{Scope of the candidate}\label{sec:scope}

We deliberately limit the scope of Conjecture~\ref{conj:envelope} to a
\textbf{cardinality} statement on the \emph{underlying group at section level}. The
conjecture does \textbf{not} claim:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Bounded derived dimensions.} Bergfalk-Lambie-Hanson
  \cite[Theorem~D]{BergfalkLH2024} show that for $S, T$ extremally disconnected
  \v{C}ech-Stone compactifications of discrete sets of cardinality $\ge \aleph_\omega$,
  the constant sheaf on $S \times T$ has \emph{infinite} injective dimension; in
  consequence, products of compact projective condensed anima are not compact. The
  cardinality envelope is not in tension with this --- it bounds underlying
  cardinalities, not Ext-tower heights.

\item \textbf{ZFC unconditionality} on the additivity of strong homology or on the
  vanishing of $\lim^n A_{\kappa,\lambda}$ for $\kappa = \lambda = \aleph_0$. These
  properties are genuinely sensitive to set-theoretic axioms
  \cite[Theorem 2.10]{BergfalkLH2024}.
\end{itemize}

The cardinality envelope is, in this sense, a \emph{first-order} structural observation
--- useful as an upper bound, but not the deepest layer of structure available in
condensed mathematics.

\section{Higher \texorpdfstring{$\pi_n$}{pi-n} extension and the K3 question (L3)}

The argument of \S\ref{sec:sketch} generalises immediately to higher condensed homotopy
groups: each $\pi_n(\Bcat\,\Pt^{\mathrm{coh}}(\mathcal{X}))(\ast)$ is a subquotient of
profinite groups indexed by points of $\mathcal{X}$. We therefore conjecture, with
confidence \textbf{L3}:

\begin{conjecture}[Higher envelope]\label{conj:higher}
For $\mathcal{X}$ spectral and $n \ge 1$,
\begin{equation}
| \pi_n(\Bcat\,\Pt^{\mathrm{coh}}(\mathcal{X}))(\ast) | \le 2^{\kappa}.
\end{equation}
\end{conjecture}

Attainment for $n \ge 2$ is open. The cleanest test case we identify is the K3 surface
over $\C$ at $n=2$, where classical
$\pi_2^{\mathrm{\acute{e}t}}(\mathrm{K3}_{\C}, \mathbf{Z}_l)$ is non-trivial of rank
$22$:

\begin{question}[K3 at $n=2$]
Does $|\pi_2^{\mathrm{cond}}(\mathrm{K3}_{\C}, \overline{x})(\ast)|$ attain $2^c$, or
does it remain at the classical rank-$22$ structure?
\end{question}

Three scenarios are possible: trivial enhancement (matches classical
$\pi_2^{\mathrm{\acute{e}t}}$), modest enhancement (continuum but not its powerset),
full saturation ($2^c$, mirroring $\pi_1$ behaviour). We are unable to resolve this in
our search, and find no published computation of $\pi_n^{\mathrm{cond}}$ for K3
surfaces at any $n \ge 2$.

\section{Falsifiability}

Conjecture~\ref{conj:envelope} is sharply falsifiable. A single attested computation of
any of the following exceeding the corresponding envelope refutes it:

\begin{itemize}
\item $|\pi_1^{\mathrm{cond}}(X_k)(\ast)|$ for some smooth qcqs scheme $X$ over a field
  $k$, exceeding $2^{|k|}$.
\item $|\GalL(T)|$ for some complete theory $T$ exceeding $2^{|\mathrm{monster}|}$.
\item A condensed Ext-cardinality at the underlying-group level exceeding $2^{|\R|}$
  in any of the regimes of \cite{BLHS2023} or \cite{BannisterBasak2026}.
\end{itemize}

We have not been able to construct any of these. If a reader can, the conjecture is
wrong, and we would be glad to know it.

\section{Author position and production method}

This preprint is produced by a non-specialist in any of the three branches it engages:
the author works at the boundary of formal methods and software engineering. The
mathematical content should be read as a \textbf{synthesis} of recently-published
results, not as expert exposition. Domain experts in condensed mathematics, model
theory, or set theory are explicitly invited to flag misreadings of any cited
literature.

The paper is the output of an autonomous experimental harness called
\emph{axiom-explorer} \cite{AxiomExplorerRepo}, which:

\begin{itemize}
\item Selects a small set of ``modern productive'' axiomatic seeds across distinct
  branches.
\item Performs a systematic bibliometric cross-search over arXiv for each pair of
  seeds (six binary combinations from four seeds; in this run, Univalence Axiom
  $\times$ Condensed Mathematics $\times$ Perfectoid Spaces $\times$ Synthetic Ricci).
\item Inventories the formal state of each seed in Mathlib4 and adjacent Lean/Agda
  projects.
\item For each surviving cross-pair, builds a dossier integrating literature,
  finite-case sanity checks (Z3, SymPy), and where applicable, a Lean 4 axiomatic
  skeleton verifying joint type consistency of the axioms involved.
\item Produces final per-phase reports and a synthesis document with explicit
  confidence levels.
\end{itemize}

The harness is LLM-assisted (the orchestrator is an LLM agent operating under explicit
stop rules, and the expert-curator role of filtering relevance is played jointly by the
LLM and the human author). All inputs and outputs of the run are versioned at the
repository in \cite{AxiomExplorerRepo} under a CC-BY-compatible license.

The candidate of this preprint emerged from the harness's Phase~3 deep dive on the
Univalence $\times$ Condensed and Condensed $\times$ Perfectoid pairs, followed by
manual cross-reading that surfaced \cite{Haine2026classifying}, \cite{BergfalkLH2024},
\cite{BLHS2023} and \cite{BannisterBasak2026}. The \textbf{unification} of the
geometric and model-theoretic sides is announced in those papers; it is not original to
this work. The \textbf{uniform cardinality envelope} \emph{across} the unification is,
to the best of our search, not formulated as such in any single source --- and is the
synthesis contribution of this preprint.
